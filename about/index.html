… also a hobby mechanic and car enthusiast.

Hi there! I’m Nishant and thank you for visiting my website.

For as long as I can remember, I have been in front of computer screens tinkering with anything that I could find. From the early days of Microsoft Paint to taking computers apart and putting them back (though mostly just leaving them there), I find solace in getting to know the working behind everyday objects.

My fascination with classic roadsters pushes me to spend my weekends trying to fix and drive them. If I don’t respond to emails within 24-hours, you can probably find me in my garage struggling to install car parts.

I taught myself web development in middle school to create a PokeBall with CSS and enjoy working on obscure projects from time-to-time. In 2016, I founded Oswald Labs — an award-winning accessibility technology company — which was borne out of my philosophy of a more equitable internet.

Over the past five years, I have worked at multiple organizations including Intuit, Searchie and Canadian Advanced Air Mobility to help them create tailored user experiences to achieve their business goals in a sustainable way.

Currently, I am studying Interactive Arts & Technology at Simon Fraser University in Vancouver, Canada.
